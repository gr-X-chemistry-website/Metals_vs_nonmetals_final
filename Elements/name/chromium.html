24.Chromium(Cr)

About Chromium

  • Chromium (Cr) is a hard, brittle, silver-gray transition metal known for its high resistance to corrosion and tarnishing due to the formation of a protective oxide layer.
  • It is an essential trace element for humans, involved in metabolism, and is widely used in producing stainless steel, as a pigment in paints and dyes, and for chrome plating.

Uses

  • Chromium uses include making stainless steel, chrome plating for corrosion resistance and shine, producing pigments for paints and ceramics, and in alloys for hardening metals.
  • It is also used in leather tanning, wood preservatives, and as a dietary mineral to aid in metabolizing fats and carbohydrates, although scientific evidence for the benefits of chromium supplements is limited.
  • Hexavalent chromium compounds are used in some industrial applications as corrosion inhibitors.

Chromium in Ancient India

  • Found in chromite deposits (Orissa belt).
  • Though India was a leader in steel production, specifically Wootz steel, which was exported globally. Chromium was not found in ancient India but was found in 1797.
