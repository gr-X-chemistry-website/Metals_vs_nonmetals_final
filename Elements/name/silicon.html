14.Silicon(Si)

About Silicon

  • Second-most abundant element in Earth's crust
  • It is not found in its free state but as silica (silicon dioxide) in sand, quartz, and rocks.

Uses

  • Highly purified silicon is used to make microchips, transistors, and other solid-state devices that are fundamental to computers and mobile phones.
  • Silicon, often in the form of silica (SiOâ‚‚), is a key component in producing glass for windows, containers, and laboratory equipment.
  • Silicon's semiconductor properties make it ideal for use in photovoltaic cells, which convert sunlight into electricity.

Silicon in Ancient India

  • Ancient Indian texts like the Arthashastra discuss chemicals, and archeological findings show the use of silica as a flux in copper smelting and as the primary component in ancient glass.
  • The Ayurvedic system, which dates back to the first millennium BC, utilizes silica in various ways, including as a substance to check for adulteration in medicines and for its potential health benefits related to lipid metabolism.
