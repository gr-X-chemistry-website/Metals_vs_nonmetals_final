21.Scandium(Sc)

About Scandium

  • Scandium (Sc) is a silvery-white, relatively soft, and rare metal that is classified as a transition metal and a rare-earth element.
  • Discovered in 1879 and named after Scandinavia, it has a high melting point and unique chemical properties, making it an important element for high-tech applications.

Uses

  • Scandium's primary uses are in creating lightweight, high-strength aluminum alloys for aerospace and sports equipment
  • Adding scandium iodide to metal halide lamps for bright, sunlight-like illumination, and utilizing its radioactive isotope, scandium-46, as a tracer in oil refining to detect pipeline leaks.
  • Other applications include electronics, analytical standards, and components for lasers and solid oxide fuel cells.

Scandium in Ancient India

  • Had no historical Indian use.
  • Identified in ore studies in modern times.
