28.Nickel(Ni)

About Nickel

  • Nickel is a hard, silvery-white, lustrous transition metal.
  • It is primarily known for its corrosion resistance, strength, and ability to form durable alloys, especially with iron and chromium to make stainless steel.

Uses

  • Nickel's main uses are in the production of stainless steel and nickel alloys.
  • These leverage its strength, heat resistance, and corrosion resistance for applications in aerospace, marine, chemical processing, and everyday products like appliances and cutlery.
  • Finely divided nickel acts as a catalyst in chemical processes, such as the hydrogenation of vegetable oils.

Nickel in Ancient India

  • Nickel's direct use in ancient India was limited but present, evidenced by a pre-Harappan axe from Nal containing 5% nickel, indicating a sophisticated understanding of metallurgy by ancient Indian smiths.
  • The primary ancient Indian connection to nickel is through its use as a component in high-quality Wootz steel, a renowned material for swords and knives.
