13.Aluminium(Al)

About Aluminium

  • Most abundant metal in earth's crust
  • It forms a protective layer of aluminum oxide when exposed to air, which prevents extensive corrosion.

Uses

  • Aluminum is used for window frames, doors, roofing, and even siding and decking, benefiting from its corrosion resistance and modern aesthetic.
  • Its ability to be impermeable and resistant to corrosion makes it perfect for food and drink cans, foil, and other wrappers, keeping food fresh.
  • The lightweight, strong, and corrosion-resistant properties of aluminum make it a staple in the construction of airplanes, spacecraft, and other vehicles.

Aluminium in Ancient India

  • Aluminium was first introduced to India in the early 20th century, initially used in prisons.
