18.Argon(Ar)

About Argon

  • Argon is the third-most abundant noble gas in Earth's atmosphere, known for its inertness, colorlessness, and odorless nature.
  • This non-reactive element is vital for uses like arc welding, where it provides a stable, oxygen-free atmosphere to prevent metal oxidation.

Uses

  • Argon uses include creating an inert atmosphere for welding and high-temperature processes, filling light bulbs to extend filament life, improving insulation in double-glazed windows, packaging food to prevent spoilage, and providing a protective gas in the electronics industry for manufacturing semiconductors.
  • Its inert, non-flammable properties make it valuable in many industries where protection from oxidation and reaction is crucial.
  • Argon provides a protective, inert atmosphere essential for the production of semiconductors and microchips, preventing unwanted reactions with moisture and oxygen.

Argon in Ancient India

  • Had no pre-modern recognition
  • First handled in Indian science after 1894
